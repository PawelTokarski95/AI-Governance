**What did we want to do?** 
As a relatively new lending company, we decided to improve the way we grant loans to individual customers. We hired a team of Data Science specialists and gave them a task: build a modern ML model using XGBoost. The model was supposed to learn from our existing historical customer database and more accurately predict who would pay back their loan and who wouldn’t.
**What went wrong and what were the consequences?** 
Instead of profits, we faced a huge disappointment. Our financial results for the last quarter were terrible – much worse than in previous months. The company started losing money rapidly because the customers approved by the new model simply stopped paying their loans back.
The management board was in shock and immediately stopped the project. The regulator quickly became interested in the case as well, because a bitter truth came to light: our company had fallen victim to a cyberattack. Hackers found vulnerabilities in our security and quietly changed the data in our database.
Our Data Science team consisted of less experienced employees. They were so excited about how well the model performed on their computers that they didn’t notice the algorithm was learning from fake profiles planted by criminals. As a result, the model viewed the fraudsters as "ideal customers" and started approving company money for them automatically. Due to this lack of data control, the company broke EU regulations, specifically the AI Act, and had to pay massive fines on top of the losses from unpaid loans.
Shortly after, we learned what should have been done to eliminate this kind of problem...
