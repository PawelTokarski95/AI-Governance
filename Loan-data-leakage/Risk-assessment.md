## Risk 1: Cyberattack on Data Infrastructure
    • Description: The risk of unauthorized access to the company's systems (databases, APIs, ETL pipelines, or the ML environment), 
    leading to the modification, theft, or replacement of data used in the credit scoring process. This attack can result in both personal data breaches 
    and data manipulation (data tampering / data poisoning).
    • Likelihood: MEDIUM (3/5) – Systems processing credit data are highly attractive targets for attackers, especially given API exposure and complex data pipelines.
    • Impact: VERY HIGH (5/5) – Possible consequences include unauthorized access to customer data, training data contamination (data poisoning),
    incorrect credit decisions generated by the model, GDPR violations (personal data leaks), financial and reputational losses, and regulatory sanctions.
    • Risk Score: HIGH (15/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Basic IAM mechanisms (role-based access control),
        ◦ Firewalls and network security measures,
        ◦ System access logging,
        ◦ Basic data encryption in databases.
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of advanced threat detection (SIEM / anomaly detection),
        ◦ Lack of segmentation for critical components of the ML pipeline,
        ◦ No monitoring of unusual data operations,
        ◦ Lack of protection against data tampering in the ETL pipeline,
        ◦ Lack of continuous security monitoring for APIs.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Deploy SIEM (Security Information and Event Management),
        ◦ Implement network segmentation (zero trust architecture),
        ◦ Monitor for anomalies in data access,
        ◦ Implement dataset signing and checksums (data integrity verification),
        ◦ API hardening (rate limiting, authentication, WAF),
        ◦ Conduct regular penetration tests and red teaming.
    • Responsible Units: Security Team (SOC), DevSecOps Team, Data Engineering Team, Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), Compliance / DPO.
## Risk 2: Improper Data Selection and Quality
    • Description: The risk of using incomplete, inconsistent, or unverified data from multiple sources. This can lead to violations of personal data
    processing principles under GDPR and incorrect credit decisions generated by the ML model.
    • Likelihood: MEDIUM (3/5) – Data comes from multiple sources (internal and external APIs), which increases the risk of inconsistencies and poor data quality.
    • Impact: HIGH (5/5) – Possible consequences include incorrect credit decisions, GDPR violations, risk of fines up to EUR 20 million or 4% of global turnover,
    and loss of customer trust.
    • Risk Score: HIGH (15/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Data validation,
        ◦ ETL controls,
        ◦ Database access restrictions,
        ◦ Process logging.
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of full data lineage,
        ◦ Lack of data anomaly detection.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Implement data lineage tracking (MLflow),
        ◦ Establish a cautious data approval process,
        ◦ Conduct periodic data compliance reviews.
    • Responsible Units: 
        Data Engineering Team (operational), 
        Chief Data Officer (business ownership), 
        Compliance / DPO (GDPR compliance).
## Risk 3: Data Poisoning (Training Data Contamination)
    • Description: The risk of introducing modified, incorrect, or unauthorized data into the training set, causing the ML model to learn
    incorrect patterns and generate flawed credit decisions.
    • Likelihood: MEDIUM (3/5) – The lack of full control over data sources and integration with external APIs increases the risk
    of distorted data entering the system.
    • Impact: HIGH (5/5) – Possible consequences include incorrect credit scoring, mass loan approvals for high-risk customers,
    financial losses, and regulatory risk.
    • Risk Score: HIGH (15/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Basic input data validation,
        ◦ Access control to the data pipeline,
        ◦ Logging of ETL processes.
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of data source verification (trusted sources only),
        ◦ Lack of anomaly and outlier detection in datasets,
        ◦ No dataset versioning.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Validate datasets before model training,
        ◦ Implement anomaly detection at the data level,
        ◦ Establish a data source whitelist,
        ◦ Implement dataset versioning (DVC / MLflow).
    • Responsible Units:
        Data Engineering Team (operational), 
        Data Science Team (training data validation), 
        Chief Data Officer (data governance), Compliance / DPO (regulatory compliance).
## Risk 4: Model Drift
    • Description: Changes in the input data distribution or the relationships between input variables and the target
    variable over time, leading to the performance degradation of the credit scoring model.
    • Likelihood: HIGH (4/5) – Changing market conditions, shifting customer behaviors, and macroeconomic factors.
    • Impact: MEDIUM (3/5) – Gradual degradation of scoring quality and an increase in the default rate.
    • Risk Score: MEDIUM (12/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Periodic model monitoring,
        ◦ Basic performance metrics tracking (AUC, accuracy).
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of automated drift detection,
        ◦ No real-time alerting,
        ◦ Lack of systematic retraining.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Implement automated drift alerting,
        ◦ Establish a cyclical model retraining schedule.
    • Responsible Units: 
        Data Science Team, 
        ML Engineering Team, 
        Model Risk Management Team.
## Risk 5: Lack of Production Model Monitoring
    • Description: The absence of continuous post-deployment monitoring of the model's performance,
    leading to delayed detection of drops in prediction quality and incorrect credit decisions.
    • Likelihood: HIGH (4/5) – Lack of a comprehensive observability system for ML models in the production environment.
    • Impact: HIGH (4/5) – Undetected deterioration of model quality leading to financial losses and regulatory risks.
    • Risk Score: HIGH (16/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Manual reporting of model results,
        ◦ Periodic performance analysis.
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of real-time monitoring,
        ◦ Absence of ML observability dashboards,
        ◦ No alerting for prediction anomalies.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Implement an ML monitoring system (drift + performance),
        ◦ Create dashboards for data and model metrics,
        ◦ Establish real-time alerting,
        ◦ Integrate with the incident response system.
    • Responsible Units: 
        ML Engineering Team, 
        Data Science Team, 
        MLOps Team.
## Risk 6: Flawed Model Validation (Offline vs. Production Mismatch)
    • Description: The risk where a model achieves high metrics in a testing environment, 
    but its actual effectiveness in production is significantly lower.
    • Likelihood: HIGH (4/5) – Gaps between training data and real-world data are common in credit scoring systems.
    • Impact: HIGH (4/5) – Incorrect evaluation of model quality, leading to the deployment of a loss-generating system.
    • Risk Score: HIGH (16/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Train/test split,
        ◦ Basic backtesting,
        ◦ Offline metrics tracking (AUC, accuracy).
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of robust temporal backtesting,
        ◦ No production simulation (shadow mode),
        ◦ Lack of validation on out-of-time data.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Implement temporal backtesting (time-series validation),
        ◦ Deploy models in shadow mode first,
        ◦ Establish a challenger model framework,
        ◦ Run out-of-time validation (OOT testing).
    • Responsible Units: 
        Data Science Team, 
        Model Risk Management Team, 
        ML Engineering Team.
## Risk 7: Model Bias / Unfairness
    • Description: The risk that the credit model generates disproportionately unfavorable decisions for specific
    customer groups, leading to violations of equal treatment principles and regulatory risks.
    • Likelihood: MEDIUM (3/5) – Bias can stem from historical data patterns or unmonitored proxy features within the model.
    • Impact: HIGH (5/5) – Severe reputational, regulatory, and legal risks.
    • Risk Score: HIGH (15/25)
    • Current Controls:
        ◦ Basic feature importance analysis,
        ◦ Manual review of models,
        ◦ Limited segment analysis.
    • Control Gaps:
        ◦ Lack of formal fairness metrics,
        ◦ No bias testing between different groups,
        ◦ Lack of post-deployment fairness monitoring.
    • Mitigation Actions:
        ◦ Implement formal fairness metrics,
        ◦ Conduct bias testing prior to deployment,
        ◦ Monitor fairness in production,
        ◦ Actively identify and remove proxy variables.
    • Responsible Units: 
        Data Science Team, 
        Model Risk Management Team, 
        Compliance / DPO.
